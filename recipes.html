    Bread

    Ezekial

    Ezekiel is a sprouted grain bread that can be purchased at health food stores and some supermarkets. If a sprouted grain bread is not your preference, opt for rye breads or those that do not contain white flour.

    Pancakes

    Bananas, Eggs, Chia Seeds

    Mash 3/4 of 1 large banana. Add 2 eggs and combine. Add 1 tbs of chia seeds to help the mixture stay together. (Chia seeds are optional, if you don’t have them the pancakes will still form and taste great!) Heat a frying pan over high heat. Add a dash of olive oil or coconut oil and cook and flip pancakes as usual! Top with a little raw honey, organic yoghurt and the remaining 1/4 of banana. You can also add berries, seeds and nuts to this delicious stack.

    Peanut Butter

    Natural Nut Spreads

    Nut spreads can be found at health food stores and supermarkets. Ensure to check the ingredients and don’t be fooled with the health claims that may be found on the front label. The spread should contain no added sugar.

    Lunch and Dinner

    Burger Bun

    Flat Mushrooms

    Peel or wipe 2 large mushrooms. Place on a tray and season with tamari, salt and pepper. Bake on 180C for 20-25 minutes depending on size of mushrooms. Remove, cool and fill with desired burger fillings.

    Chicken Nuggets

    Chicken Breast, Coconut Flour, Shredded Coconut, Eggs

    Cut 500g of chicken breast in to bite size pieces and roll in 1 cup of coconut flour. Then dip the chicken into 2 beaten eggs and coat the chicken with 1 1/2 cups of shredded coconut. Place on baking tray and cook for 20-25 minutes on 180C.

    Lasagne

    Eggplant or Zucchini

    Using a wide vegetable peeler, slice either zucchini or eggplant to create strips. These will be used to replace the sheets of pasta. Layer your sauce and separate with the desired amount of vegetable strips to cover the tray.

    Mash Potato

    Cauliflower, Butter, Cream, Cheddar

    Steam the 1 head of cauliflower for 5 minutes or until tender. Combine cauliflower with 20g of butter,1/4 cup of cream and 1 cup of cheese in a blender and blend until smooth. Season with salt and pepper.

    Pizza Base

    Cauliflower, Egg, Olive Oil

    Pulse half a head of steamed or raw cauliflower, to reach a rice like texture. Place the mixture in a tea towel or cloth and squeeze out as much moisture as possible. In a mixing bowel, combine cauliflower, 1 egg, 1tsp of olive oil and herbs and spices if desired. Line a baking tray with banking paper and spread mixture to be approximately 1.5cm thick. Bake on 180C for 20-25 minutes.

    Rice

    Cauliflower

    Wash 1 head of cauliflower. Break into florets and place in food processor. Pulse for a few seconds until you have achieved a rice like texture. Optional you may add lemon juice, salt, pepper, herbs and spices. Alternatively, brown rice is healthier compared to white rice.

    Spaghetti

    Zucchini or Carrot

    Using a spiralizer simply spiral either 1 carrot, zucchini or both into a bowl. Top with your pasta sauce. Replacing regular pasta with vegetables is highly beneficial. Pasta is high in carbohydrates, which can often make us feel hungry not long after we have eaten. By using vegetables instead of pasta there are far fewer carbohydrates and you will feel fuller for longer.

    Taco Shells

    Capsicum

    Prepare your taco filling as usual. Instead of using taco shells, stuff half capsicums with filling and place in oven for 15 minutes on 180C. Top with salads.

    Wedges/Fries

    Sweet Potato

    Chop 1 large sweet potato into medium or thin chunks. Top with either olive or coconut oil. Season with sea salt, pepper, cinnamon, Harissa or other spices. Bake for 50 - 60 minutes on 180C.

    Snacks and Desserts

    Chocolate

    Cacao Powder, Coconut Oil, Raw Honey

    Combine 1 cup of cacao powder, 2Tbsp of raw honey and 1 cup of coconut oil. Ensure coconut oil is melted. Pour into chocolate moulds and allow to set in the fridge for a few hours.

    Fruit Flavoured Yoghurt

    Natural Yoghurt, Fruit, Nuts and Seeds

    Prepare your desired fruits by shopping them into bite size pieces. Mix fruit, nuts and seeds through natural yoghurt. Additionally, you could blend the fruit with the yoghurt if you would prefer a smoother consistency.

    Ice Cream

    Frozen Fruit, Almond Milk

    Combine frozen fruit, such as berries and bananas with 1 cup of almond milk. Blend until you achieve a soft serve consistency.

    Mousse

    Avocado, Cacao Powder, Coconut Oil, Raw Honey

    Add 1-2 avocados, 1 heaped tbs of cacao (or cocoa) powder, 1 heaped tbs melted coconut oil, 1 tbs raw honey to blender. Blend until smooth and glossy. More cacao or honey can be added to taste.

    Potato Crisps

    Kale, Coconut Oil, Salt

    Break medium-sized pieces off kale from 1 bunch and dip into 1tsp of coconut oil along with a sprinkle of salt. Spread kale on a baking tray and cook on 130C for approximately 10 minutes, or until the edges brown lightly. Alternatively, spices can be added to the kale before cooking.
